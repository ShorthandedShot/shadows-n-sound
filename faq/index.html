How does the converter work?
The SHADOWS >>> SOUND converter generates audio based off of visuals of shadows that are uploaded. The converter takes a shadow's outline and generates an audio file that is based on the found outline.
what file types are supported?
SHADOWS >>> SOUND supports .jpg, .png, .mp4, .mov, and .wmv file types. It is reccommended that users upload .jpg or .png files.

Only a single frame of a video file will be used to generate audio. This frame will be selected at random according by the SHADOWS >>> SOUND converter.
what kind of visuals should i upload?
Visuals that are uploaded should include clear, defined shadows that have a visible outline. The shadows can be anything, and there are no limitations.

Shadows that have less defined outlines, are muddy, or overlapping with multiple other shadows will have more varied results and audio quality.
can i get more than one sound from an image?
The converter in it's current state is only capable of generating one sound per visual. However, if an image is cropped and has a different outline, this can lead to a differently generated audio track.
